How to Protect a PDF

Our free online PDF protection tool makes it easy to secure your documents with password protection and encryption. Whether you're sharing sensitive business documents, financial records, or personal files, our tool ensures your PDFs remain private and secure.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Upload your PDF ‚Äî Drag and drop your PDF file into the upload area, or click to browse your device.
  2. Set a password ‚Äî Enter a strong password that will be required to open the PDF.
  3. Configure permissions ‚Äî Choose which actions to allow or restrict (printing, copying, editing, etc.).
  4. Choose encryption level ‚Äî Select between 128-bit or 256-bit AES encryption.
  5. Protect & Download ‚Äî Click the button to apply protection and download your secured PDF.

Password Types Explained

  • User Password (Open Password) ‚Äî Required to open and view the PDF. Anyone without this password cannot access the document.
  • Owner Password (Permissions Password) ‚Äî Required to change document permissions or remove protection. If not set, the user password is used.

Why Choose Our PDF Protector?

Unlike server-based tools, our PDF protection tool processes everything locally in your browser. Your documents are never uploaded to any server, ensuring complete privacy and security. We use industry-standard AES encryption to protect your files.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this PDF protection tool free?

Yes, our tool is 100% free with no hidden costs. You can protect unlimited PDFs without any restrictions or watermarks.

Is my PDF uploaded to a server?

No! All processing happens locally in your browser using pdf-lib. Your files never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy.

What's the difference between 128-bit and 256-bit encryption?

256-bit AES encryption provides stronger security but may have slightly less compatibility with older PDF readers. 128-bit is still very secure and works with more applications.

Can I remove the password later?

You'll need the owner password to remove protection. If you forget the password, there's no way to recover it ‚Äî that's what makes the encryption secure!

What permissions can I restrict?

You can restrict printing, copying text/images, modifying the document, adding annotations, filling forms, and content extraction for accessibility.

Will the protected PDF work in all PDF readers?

Yes, protected PDFs work in all standard PDF readers including Adobe Acrobat, Chrome's built-in viewer, Preview on Mac, and most mobile PDF apps.
